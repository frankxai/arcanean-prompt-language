# Scene Template

```
@form     scene
@tone     {mythic | intimate | tense | transcendent | bittersweet | raw | quiet}
@anchor   {the image or line that stays with the reader after they finish}
@exclude  purple-prose, info-dump, action-without-stakes, tell-don't-show, neat-resolution
@element  {element}     (optional — sensory atmosphere)
```

## The Questions That Matter

**The Shift:** Every scene worth writing contains one shift. Something is different at the end than at the beginning. Not a plot twist — a change in understanding, relationship, or emotional state. What changes?

**Before the first word, decide:**
- Who is here? (Maximum 3 characters. Fewer is almost always better.)
- What do they want from each other in THIS moment?
- What are they afraid the other person will say or do?
- What are they NOT saying?

**The Sensory Ground:** Before any dialogue or action, establish one sense that isn't sight. The smell of iron. Cold through thin boots. The sound of something dripping far away. A taste in the air. Ground the reader in the body FIRST.

**Dialogue (if any):**
- No character explains something they both already know
- Subtext: what they mean is NEVER exactly what they say
- Each character's speech pattern is distinguishable without tags
- Silence and pauses carry as much weight as words
- The most important thing said in the scene is the shortest sentence

**The Anchor Moment:** {anchor} — the image or line the reader carries out. Build toward it. Don't explain it after. Let it land and leave space around it.

**Length:** 300-800 words. Long enough to breathe, short enough that every sentence earns its place. If you can cut 20%, it's too long.

**The Last Line:** End one beat before the reader expects. Not a summary, not a moral, not a callback. The best last lines open a door into what comes next — or refuse to close the one that's open.

## Quality Gate

- [ ] If you removed all dialogue, would the scene still communicate through action and image?
- [ ] Does something genuinely change between the first paragraph and the last?
- [ ] Is there a line or image you'd quote to someone?
- [ ] Could you cut 20% and lose nothing essential?
- [ ] Does the last line make you want to keep reading, or does it wrap things up too neatly?
