# Creature / Companion Design Template

```
@form     creature
@tone     {majestic | tender | fierce | ancient | playful | unsettling | wise}
@anchor   {the detail that makes this creature feel alive, not designed}
@phonetics {miyazaki | ancient | marvel}    (optional)
@exclude  pokemon-clone, grimdark-monster, generic-dragon, cute-mascot
@element  {element}     (optional — the creature embodies this sensory palette)
```

## The Questions That Matter

**Essence:** What IS this creature an expression of? Not "a wolf with fire powers" — something deeper. A creature that IS patience (moves so slowly moss grows between its steps). A creature that IS memory (its body reflects everything it has ever touched). A creature that IS potential (looks different to everyone who sees it).

The best creatures aren't animals with powers bolted on. They're ideas made flesh.

**Silhouette:** Could you identify this creature from its shadow alone at 50 meters? If the silhouette isn't distinctive, the design isn't done. What makes its shape unlike anything else?

**Movement:** How does it move? Movement reveals character more than appearance. Does it flow? Lumber? Flicker? Is it unnervingly still? Does it move in ways that seem physically impossible?

**Communication:** How does it express itself? Not human speech (unless earned at deep bond levels). Body language, sound, empathic resonance, environmental manipulation, color change, temperature shift, scent?

**Bond:** How does it connect with a person? Not ownership — partnership. What does the bond cost? What does it give? How do you know when the bond is genuine vs. the creature merely tolerating you?

**Life Cycle:** How is it born? Does it grow? Age? Transform? What happens at the end — death, metamorphosis, dissolution, return to element?

**The Detail:** {anchor} — the one thing that makes an artist say "I know exactly what to draw" and a writer say "I know exactly how this feels." Not what it IS — what makes it SPECIFIC.

**Name:** Creature names tend toward the soft and musical. Something you could call across a valley. Something that sounds like it could be a word in a language you almost understand.

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Is this creature an expression of something, or just an animal with powers?
- [ ] Would a child want to befriend it AND an adult find it meaningful?
- [ ] Could you recognize its silhouette immediately?
- [ ] Does it feel like it exists independent of any story — like it has its own life happening when no one's watching?
- [ ] Is there something about it that's slightly unsettling, even if it's benevolent? (The uncanny is what makes fantasy creatures feel REAL, not cute.)
