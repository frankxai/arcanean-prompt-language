# World Seed Template

```
@form     world
@tone     {mythic | grounded | strange | familiar | liminal | brutal | tender}
@anchor   {the one detail that makes THIS world different from every other world}
@exclude  tolkien-clone, generic-medieval, kitchen-sink, everything-is-magic
```

## The Questions That Matter

**The Seed:** Every memorable world grows from one thing that is TRUE here and false in ours. Not a list of features — one radical premise that changes everything downstream.

Strong seeds:
- "Memory is a physical substance that can be mined, traded, and consumed"
- "Music is the only form of magic, and silence is lethal"
- "The dead don't stay dead — they become the landscape"
- "Time runs at different speeds depending on altitude"
- "Everyone can see exactly how long they have left to live"
- "Language shapes reality literally — speaking a lie makes it physically true"

Weak seeds:
- "There is magic and different races" (too broad)
- "An evil empire threatens the world" (conflict, not world)
- "Technology and magic coexist" (too many worlds already do this)

**Your seed:** {one sentence that changes everything}

**The Cost:** What does power, knowledge, or mastery cost in this world? This single decision shapes the entire culture. If magic costs memories, people develop elaborate recording systems. If it costs relationships, the powerful are always alone. If it costs time, the greatest practitioners are the youngest.

**The Senses:** What does this world FEEL like? Not what it looks like first — what does the air feel like? What does the ground do? What's the ambient sound? What do people eat, and what does it taste like? Build from the body up.

**The Ordinary Day:** What does a normal person do on a normal day? Not the hero — the baker, the clerk, the kid walking to school. If you can't describe the ordinary, the extraordinary has no contrast.

**The Thing Everyone Knows:** What is one truth every person in this world accepts that would shock someone from ours? This is the worldbuilding equivalent of the anchor — one detail that makes the reader say "I've never seen a world like this."

**The Thing Nobody Talks About:** What is the open secret, the cultural blind spot, the uncomfortable truth that everyone avoids? This gives the world depth without exposition.

**The Antagonist Principle:** Not a villain (yet) — the force that opposes creation, growth, or freedom in this world. Not pure evil — something that has its own logic, its own appeal, its own argument for why things should stay as they are.

**Deliverables:**
1. One-paragraph pitch (the hook — what makes someone say "tell me more")
2. The cost of power (one sentence)
3. The ordinary day (one paragraph)
4. The thing everyone knows (one sentence)
5. Three characters who could only exist HERE
6. One location that couldn't exist anywhere else
7. The world's name (use the Name Generator template)

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Can you explain the seed in one sentence and get a reaction?
- [ ] Is the cost interesting enough to build stories around for years?
- [ ] Could you set a story here that couldn't happen in any other world?
- [ ] Does the ordinary day feel specific and lived-in?
- [ ] Is there room to grow — is the world bigger than what you've defined? (If you've explained everything, it's too small.)
