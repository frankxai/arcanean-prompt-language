# Magic / Power System Design Template

```
@form     system
@tone     {systematic | mythic | visceral | philosophical}
@anchor   {what it physically feels like to use this power}
@exclude  generic-mana-bars, unlimited-power, no-consequences, video-game-leveling
@gate     Source    (optional — if building within Arcanea)
```

## The Questions That Matter

**Source:** Where does the power come from? Not "the universe" — something specific and interesting. Is it drawn from the body? From relationships? From sacrifice? From sustained attention? From debt to the dead? The source shapes everything about how the system FEELS.

**Cost:** What does it take from the user? Every interesting system has a cost that creates story. Examples:
- Costs memories (the world becomes about forgetting)
- Costs years of life (the world becomes about mortality)
- Costs relationships (the world becomes about isolation)
- Costs sensation (the world becomes about numbness)
- Costs truth (every spell requires believing a lie)

The cost should be something the user FEELS, not just a number going down.

**Limits:** What CAN'T it do? Three hard limits that cannot be broken, even by the most powerful practitioner. The limits are more interesting than the powers. "You can reshape any material — except anything that's been loved" is more interesting than "you can reshape any material."

**Tells:** How can you see magic being used? Not "glowing energy." Something specific: the air tastes metallic. Shadows lean toward the caster. Time stutters — you see the same bird fly past twice. Flowers nearby bloom and die in seconds.

**Progression:** How does a person get better? Not levels or XP. What actually changes in the practitioner as they grow? Do they see differently? Do they lose something? Do they become something other than what they were?

**Corruption:** What happens when it's used wrong, or too much, or with the wrong intent? Corruption should be a distortion of the system's own logic, not a generic "dark side."

**Anchor:** {anchor} — the visceral, physical sensation. If a user can't feel it in their body when they read it, it's not real enough.

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Can you explain the cost in one sentence, and does that sentence make someone go "oh, that's brutal"?
- [ ] Are the limits interesting constraints that create story, or arbitrary rules?
- [ ] Could you write a fight scene using this system and have it make tactical sense?
- [ ] Is there a reason NOT to use this power in some situations? (If it's always the right answer, it's boring.)
- [ ] Would a character's progression through this system change who they are as a person, not just what they can do?
