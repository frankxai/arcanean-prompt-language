# Name Generation Template

```
@form     name
@tone     {ancient | elegant | sharp | musical | heavy | strange}
@phonetics {tolkien | marvel | miyazaki | ancient | slavic | arabic | celtic | nordic}
@anchor   {what the name should FEEL like when spoken aloud}
@exclude  apostrophe-abuse, unpronounceable, existing-franchise-names, generic-fantasy
```

## The Questions That Matter

**What is being named?** Character, location, artifact, creature, organization, magic concept, world?

**Phonetic Personality:** Names carry sound-meaning even before you know what they mean:
- **Hard consonants** (K, D, T, G): Power, impact, authority. Names that CRACK.
- **Liquid consonants** (L, R, V, Y): Flow, grace, beauty. Names that POUR.
- **Nasals** (M, N, NG): Weight, groundedness, warmth. Names that HUM.
- **Fricatives** (S, SH, F, TH): Mystery, danger, edge. Names that WHISPER.
- **Aspirated** (H, WH, breathy vowels): Freedom, spirit, lightness. Names that FLOAT.
- **Unusual combos** (ZH, KI, QU, AE): Strangeness, otherness, alien beauty. Names that PAUSE you.

**Syllable Rules:**
- 1 syllable: Punchy, iconic. Works for titles or shortened forms. (Sol, Flux, Wraith)
- 2 syllables: Strong, memorable. The sweet spot for most names. (Leyla, Kyuro, Atlas)
- 3 syllables: Elegant, formal. Good for full names or places. (Draconia, Vaelith, Veloura)
- 4 syllables: Grand, mythic. Use sparingly. (Lyssandria, Shinkami — wait, that's 3. Good instinct.)
- 5+: Almost never. If you need this many syllables, the name isn't working.

**Stress Pattern:** Should feel natural to an English speaker. Stress on the wrong syllable makes a name feel like a mispronunciation, not a name.

**Generate:**
- 5 primary options (best candidates)
- 3 variations of the strongest (short form, formal form, ancient/intimate form)
- 1 wild card that breaks one of the rules in an interesting way

**For each name:**
- Pronunciation: stress-marked (LYS-san-dria, not lys-SAN-dri-a)
- Why it works: which phonetic choices carry the meaning
- What it feels like: weight? speed? old? bright? unsettling?

## Common Traps

- **Apostrophe names** (Kal'thar, D'arklon): Almost always bad. The apostrophe adds nothing.
- **-ia/-ius endings on everything**: Latin defaults. Use sparingly.
- **Combining two English words** (Shadowbane, Stormrider, Darkwood): Lazy. Always lazy.
- **Names from published franchises**: Check. Always check.
- **Accidentally real words in other languages**: Also check. "Kuso" means something in Japanese.

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Say each name aloud three times. Did you stumble? Cut it.
- [ ] Would you remember this name after hearing it once in conversation?
- [ ] Does it sound like it could exist alongside names you already love?
- [ ] Is there at least one option that surprised you?
- [ ] Does the name SOUND like the thing it names? (A fragile character shouldn't have a name that sounds like a warhammer.)
